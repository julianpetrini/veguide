Gordon Ramsay

After finishing his studies, Ramsay placed himself under the direction of some of Europe's top chefs. He apprenticed with Marco Pierre White at Harvey's in London, worked for Albert Roux at Le Gavroche, and then worked under master chefs Joel Robuchon and Guy Savoy in France. In 1993, Ramsay struck out on his own as head chef of the newly opened Aubergine in London where, over the course of three years, he earned the restaurant a two-star rating from Michelin. His restaurants are hailed as a destinations for high-end foodies and is now sharing this experience with you. Enjoy high level of technique in a homey gathering while you savour our dishes.

Antoni Porowski

Dinner with Antoni means satisfying meals with loads of vegetables, with an occasional carb splurge. Antoni knows how to crank the flavor, make exciting suppers and create new takes on classics by swapping in a surprising ingredient or two. Simple, yes, but always special. Porowski is a self-taught gourmet and avid home cook, He’s worked in restaurants and as a sommelier and personal chef.

Daniela Soto-Innes

Daniela Soto-Innes has always been surrounded by strong women who love cooking. She is a Mexican-born chef and the youngest chef named World's Best Female Chef by the World's 50 Best Restaurant. Daniela Soto-Innes is convinced that emotion, spice, and an irrepressible lust for life go hand in hand in Mexican culinary culture. Her expert tip: “If you want to prepare mole or tamales, you need to be happy – otherwise it will never turn out right. Making a party out of fine dining is another one of Soto-Innes’ goals, who, with tacos and flax seed chilaquiles as weapons, is shaking the idea that formality and protocol are a requirement for haute cuisine.
